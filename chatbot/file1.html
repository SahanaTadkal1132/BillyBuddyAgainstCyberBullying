What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is a form of harassment on a social/online platform. It has become very common, especially among teenagers. Harmful bullying behavior can include posting rumors, threats, sexual remarks, a victim’s personal information, or pejorative labels (i.e., hate speech). Bullying or harassment can be identified by repeated behavior and an intent to harm. Victims may have lower self-esteem, increased suicidal ideation, and a variety of emotional responses, including being scared, frustrated, angry, and depressed.

How Our Website Helps

Our Website provides instant help through a user-friendly chatbot (“Billy”) which not only comforts the victim but also reports the person behind the bullying to the cyber-crime department, keeping the user’s identity anonymous. It helps victims provide important information and evidence, and calculates cyber-crime statistics in an area, visible to the department to keep track of crimes regularly. Areas with a red alert will be given more focus across the country.
